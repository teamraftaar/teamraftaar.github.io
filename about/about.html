About Us

Team Raftaar is a team of undergraduate students of Delhi Technological University who share a common vision, i.e., to engrain the dynamic concept of Human Powered Vehicles in our day to day lives and thus, promote sustainable modes of transportation for a better world ahead. For the same, we design a fully Carbon Fibre faired recumbent bicycle to optimize the aerodynamic and drivetrain related traits which has bolstered our capabilities to achieve speeds in excess of 80 kmph purely out of human power.

Manufacturing our Human Powered Vehicle completely out of Carbon Fibre makes our Vehicle lighter and faster than ever. With a fully faired carbon fibre vehicle coupled with enhanced manoeuvrability and robustness, we believe in making this innovation a commercially viable alternative.

Having established our stronghold in the Indian [2015] and the Asia Paciﬁc [2017] events by being the Overall champions, we participated in the North American Event and by working on a “Metal-Free” Human Powered Vehicle and manufacturing it completely out of Carbon Fiber making our Vehicle lighter and faster than ever we secured 5th Position in Design Event at the ASME HPVC E-Fest 2018, East Coast held at Penn State University, Pennsylvania, USA.

Our electronics team developed a Landing Gear Suggestion System (LGSS) for a vehicle that ensures safety along with optimal performance which was awarded 1st prize at the Penn State University, Pennsylvania, USA.

ACHIEVEMENTS

Team Raftaar is the ﬁrst Indian Human Powered Vehicle team to design and fabricate a Metal-Free Human Powered Vehicle.

Secured 1st position in analysis and 5th in design in ASME E fest 2019 at VIT Vellore.
Winners of the innovation category at ASME E-Fest HPVC 2018 North America at Pennsylvania state university, USA. Also secured the 5th position in design.
Secured 1st position in men’s and women’s drag race and 3rd position in design at ASME HPVC Asia pacific 2018.Also secured the overall runners up position.
Overall winners of the ASME HPVC Asia Pacific 2015 and 2017.
Secured overall 12th position in ASME HPVC North America west 2016.
Successfully built the fastest human powered vehicle in India with a top speed of 80 kmph.
